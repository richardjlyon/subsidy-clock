#RecipientTechnologySchemeCumulative

Wind farms built in stages hold a separate CfD contract per phase; these rows group the phases into one physical station, which you can expand to see the individual contracts (for example Walney Extension Phase 1 and Phase 2). Station groupings follow the LCCC contract register, via David Turver. Renewables Obligation recipients are shown at buy-out value — the per-generator basis, from David Turver’s ROC reconstruction; the RO scheme total additionally includes recycle value, so these per-station figures understate the full cost. A plant with units under more than one scheme appears once per scheme — Drax, for example, holds Renewables Obligation biomass units and a separate CfD unit, shown as two rows. Constraint-payment recipients reflect the bottom-up settlement window shown in the ‘Paid to switch off’ section; earlier constraint history is held at annual totals only.

‘Lead party’ is the registered Balancing Mechanism party that routes a unit’s output to market, not necessarily the asset’s owner. One lead party (for example Statkraft) may trade on behalf of several wind farms, so it appears as the payee for constraint payments it passes through to them.
