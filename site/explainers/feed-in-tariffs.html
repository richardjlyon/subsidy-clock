What is it?

Feed-in Tariffs paid households, farms and businesses for generating their own renewable electricity — mostly rooftop solar panels. Between 2010 and 2019 anyone installing panels locked in payments for 20 to 25 years, for every unit generated, whether they used it or not. Suppliers fund those payments and pass the cost on to all billpayers — including those who could never afford panels. Payments continue into the 2040s.

Why does it exist?

Solar panels were expensive in 2010, and the government wanted ordinary households, not just energy companies, to install renewable generation. Generous guaranteed payments made small-scale solar a safe investment; the cost was spread invisibly across everyone else's bills.

What has it cost?

since 2010, currently growing at about a year — every second. Spread across Britain's households, that is about per household per year, paid through electricity bills.
